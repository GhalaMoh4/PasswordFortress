How to Improve Your Password

  • Make it longer: Aim for at least 12 characters, or even longer if possible. The longer your password, the harder it is to crack.
  • Use a mix of characters: Include uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols in your password. This makes it harder to guess or crack.
  • Avoid personal information: Don't use information that can be easily found online, such as your name, birthdate, or phone number. Hackers can use this information to guess your password.
  • Don't reuse passwords: Use a unique password for each account. If one password is compromised, it won't affect your other accounts.
  • Use a password manager: Consider using a password manager to generate and store strong passwords for you. This makes it easier to use complex passwords without having to remember them all.
